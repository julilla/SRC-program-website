Purpose of the Curriculum

The Integrated Curriculum for Socially Responsible Computing provides a comprehensive framework for teaching responsible computing throughout the computer science curriculum at Brown. This curriculum is designed to:

  • Help courses determine what to teach in terms of socially responsible computing
  • Reduce duplication of SRC content across courses
  • Establish the SRC-related skills that a Brown CS graduate should have
  • Allow courses to build on SRC topics across courses and deepen the quality of content

Five Levels of Skills

The curriculum uses five distinct levels of learning objectives to scaffold student understanding and skill development:

Students have heard of a concept/idea and how it relates to a given technical context. Students are able to recall the concept/idea as relevant in a specific context. ("I know X is a thing.")

Students can explain the meaning and/or significance of a specific concept/idea. They can articulate how it functions and/or why it is important.

Students are able to recognize relevant concerns in a specific technical context without prompting and are able to articulate how they would affect relevant technical decisions.

Students are able to critically evaluate a specific concept/idea, either abstractly or in a specific social or technical context. They are able to cite and engage with different concerns and/or conflicting perspectives on the topic in question.

Students are able to execute concrete technical decisions or use specific techniques to achieve a desired objective.
